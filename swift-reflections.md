# Week 1 Reflection

A work is considered E-Literature when it becomes an interactive experience. Something that is unlikely to have the same effect physically from printed literature. Its existence distinguishes how we read or 'digest' literature.

Shade was particularly interesting as it prompted a new way of discovering concepts of the written word. Frustration became a key emotion as often I found myself wanting to know more, and quickly - to find the right words. Similar to a choose-your-own-adventure, but more so presenting a route of discovery to those choices and options. A much more personal and intriging adventure, yet stuck or trapped in the puzzling mind of the author. 

For this course, I really came in with no expectations, having only seen its title and no description. I am looking to open myself up to the idea of experimentation in a field I am not completely comfortable with - to experiment with ideas and passion for a new form of expression. Having never coded before, I look forward to learning and building my own website - an important skill to have and utilise in such a technologically advancing society.
